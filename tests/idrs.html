On the Thursday following Whit Sunday the congregaon of Cripplestyle Congregaonal Church processed to the top of the hill for an open-air service. During that day, from sunrise to sunset a ﬂag ﬂew with the message “Feed My Lambs.” This was to mark the anniversary of the opening of the Ebenezer Chapel in 1807. Past forms of the name are, Kyngbor’ghe 1404 Cecil, Kingbarowe e17 CecilMap, Kingborrowe Heathe 1619 CH, King Barrow 1811 OS, King Barrowe Patch and Piece 1846 TA and King Barrow Hill 1869 Hutch. From the Old English meaning cyning ‘king’ and beorg ‘barrow or hill.’ The current Ordnance Survey name is King Barrow. The Local Defence Volunteers (LDV’s) held exercises on King Barrow. There is a legend that Uther the father of King Arthur is buried on the hill. In 1940 when the Home Guard were ‘dug-in’, no signiﬁcant ﬁnds were made. Between Thursday 28 th and Sunday 31 st May 2020 a bench was placed on the hill!
